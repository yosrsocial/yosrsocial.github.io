يسر - استراتيجية وإبداع

I came into business school wanting to work in marketing because it felt creative. Yet I left with a degree in strategic business management and a deep distrust of the industry. Then spent years treating social media management as a temporary gig while I figured out what actually felt like me.

It took a research spiral about skincare content and a growing fury at how easy it has become to produce content that looks legitimate and means nothing for me to realize that walking away wasn't the righteous move. It was the complicit one.

Marketing at its core is connecting people to genuine solutions. That definition only works if the solution is actually genuine. So I work with brands that deliver real value and help them build the kind of audience trust that no ad budget can buy and no competitor can easily break. It's easier than ever to scam. Which means honesty is now the strongest strategy there is. That's my niche. That's the mission.

Home
Talk
Industry Online Education
Market Algerian / French Diaspora
Scope Brand Identity + Social Media Strategy + Community Management
Year 2026
The Situation
Before we started working together, the online presence of this brand was all over the place. You couldn't figure out what services they provided or even what industry they were a part of by glancing at their profile. the name and logo of the brand made it seem like a culinary school when it was actually a language school.

That was because the client was trying to target a very large and unspecified audience. Some people are under the impression the more generic their content is the more people they'll appeal to, thus making more sales. When in fact the opposite is true.
The Approach
I hopped on a strategy call with the client, mapped their goals and current services they offer, then planned a full rebrand complete with the visual identity and the content strategy. The main decision we worked on was finding a niche, in this client's case was offering online language tutoring to Algerian children between 6-16 years old, by teachers who live in the countries where the language is spoken in.'
